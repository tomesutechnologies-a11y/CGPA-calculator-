Frequently Asked Questions

What is CGPA?
CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It represents the weighted mean of all your grade points across every semester throughout your academic program. Unlike a single-semester GPA, CGPA reflects your overall academic performance from enrollment to date.
How is CGPA calculated?
CGPA is calculated by dividing the total quality points by the total credit units. Quality points for each course equal the grade point multiplied by the course credit unit. Sum all quality points across all semesters, then divide by the sum of all credit units. Formula: CGPA = Total Quality Points / Total Credit Units.
What is the difference between GPA and CGPA?
GPA (Grade Point Average) is calculated for a single semester or term. CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall average across all semesters you have completed. Your semester GPA contributes to your CGPA, but they are distinct metrics — one measures a term, the other measures your entire degree progress.
What grading system does this calculator use?
This calculator uses the Nigerian 5-point grading system: A (Excellent) = 5.0, B (Very Good) = 4.0, C (Good) = 3.0, D (Fair) = 2.0, E (Pass) = 1.0, F (Fail) = 0.0. This scale is standard in most Nigerian universities and similar institutions.
What CGPA do I need for First Class Honours?
Under the 5-point grading system, the classification thresholds are typically: First Class (4.50 - 5.00), Second Class Upper (3.50 - 4.49), Second Class Lower (2.40 - 3.49), Third Class (1.50 - 2.39), Pass (1.00 - 1.49). Below 1.00 is typically a Fail. These may vary slightly between institutions.
Is my data saved?
Yes. All data you enter in both Simple and Advanced modes is automatically saved to your browser's local storage. When you return to the calculator, your previous entries will be restored. This data stays on your device only and is never sent to any server.
